🧭 **Project Overview**

This project analyzes gas production performance across formations and wells, focusing on:

Production contribution by formation  
Decline behavior over time  
Water production risk (Water-Gas Ratio / WGR)  
Well-level performance diagnostics  

The objective is to identify optimization opportunities and production risks to support better asset management decisions.

**3.Optimization Candidate: Dzybon 1 (Stimulation & Lift)**

<img width="1298" height="287" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6f6d6326-1a55-40e2-9178-3d54c6a4b9f3" />

Production Efficiency Diagnostic  
Even at 20 years of age, Dzybon 1 remains one of the top 10 producers. However, the relationship between its WGR and Gas Rate reveals a classic Late-Stage Production Profile:
- Observation: As gas rates have declined to the 100K-200K range, the WGR has shown volatility with a general upward trend.
- Inference: The well is likely struggling with liquid loading. The fact that the gas rate is still relatively high despite the age and water production suggests that the reservoir energy is still present, but the wellbore is restricted by hydrostatic backpressure from produced water.

Recommendation: Stimulation & Optimization  
A. Artificial Lift Optimization (Deliquification)
- Diagnosis: The rising WGR is creating hydrostatic backpressure, "choking" the natural flow of gas.
- Advice: Implement or optimize Plunger Lift cycles. Since the gas rate is still healthy, there is enough reservoir energy to effectively "trip" a plunger, which would clear the water column and allow the gas to flow more freely.

B. Re-Perforation / Stimulation
- Advice: Since this is a carbonate/shale-related formation (Black River), check for Calcium Carbonate scale buildup. A targeted Acid Wash or re-perforation of the high-productivity intervals could restore the "skin factor" to near-zero.

  
**4. Risk Mitigation Candidate: Henkel 1359-A (Water Control)**

<img width="1298" height="287" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a00b4bc2-26e5-4a6c-8869-9008c25bc2e7" />

Diagnostic Profile
- Age: 25 Years (One of the oldest in the Top 10).
- Production Trend: It has reached a "Terminal Decline" or a flat plateau at a very low rate.
- WGR Status: A sudden, sharp increase in Water-Gas Ratio starting around 2020.


Recommendation:

A. Water Shut-Off (WSO) Evaluation:
- Diagnosis: The sudden spike in WGR at year 21 suggests a specific mechanical failure or water coning.
- Advice: Run a Production Logging Tool (PLT) to identify the specific depth of water entry. If the water is coming from a bottom perforation, consider a Cement Plugback or using Inflow Control Devices (ICD) to block the water-producing zone.
  
B. Chemical Scale Inhibition:
- Advice: At 25 years, the wellbore is likely restricted by Barium or Calcium scales due to the high water volume. A continuous chemical injection capillary string could prevent further restriction.

**5. Decommissioning Candidate: Lovell 1323 (Shutdown)**

<img width="1298" height="287" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e11f98df-b5df-498a-bdfc-653634cf654d" />

- Lovell 1323 has reached its Economic Limit ($Q_{el}$). The natural energy of the reservoir is exhausted, and the well is currently "flatlined" at its lowest production point in 25 years.
- Engineering Advice: To maintain a lean and profitable asset, this well should be Plugged and Abandoned (P&A). Continuing to operate a well with such low deliverability results in negative cash flow due to lifting costs and surface equipment maintenance.
